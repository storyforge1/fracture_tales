Tutorial: Transforming The Legend of Surabaya 🪄

Step 1: Determine The Twist

Before writing, decide what you want to fracture.
• Original: Fighting over a goat (food)
• Fractured: Fighting over a limited smartphone (modern twist)

1. Orientation

Original (LKS) Fractured Version (New)
A long time ago, there were two animals, Sura and Baya. Sura was a shark and Baya was a crocodile. They lived by the sea. A long time ago, Sura and Baya were best friends. Sura was a cool shark and Baya was a trendy crocodile. They lived in a big apartment in Surabaya city.

Lesson: We’re changing the setting from the ocean to an apartment in the city to give it a more modern feel.

2. Complication

Original (LKS) Fractured Version (New)
Once Sura and Baya were looking for some food. Suddenly, Baya saw a goat. They fought for the goat. Later, Sura broke the promise and went to the river. Baya was furious and they fought again fiercely. Once Sura and Baya were looking for a new gadget. Suddenly, Baya saw a limited edition "Gold Phone". Sura wanted it too, so they argued loudly. One day, Sura broke their agreement and bought all the phone cases at the mall. Baya was furious when he knew it.

Lesson: We changed the subject of the story from “Goat” to “Gold Phone” and the setting to a mall, but we still used the simple past tense (V2).

3. Resolution

Original (LKS) Fractured Version (New)
Sura finally gave up and went back to the sea. Baya was happy. They did not hit each other. Instead, they had a TikTok dance battle to decide the winner. Sura lost and went home. Baya was happy.

Lesson: In the Fractured version, we can turn physical action (fighting) into something funny, like a dance battle.
