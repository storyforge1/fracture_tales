Simple Past Tense

Simple past tense is a form of sentence that states an action or event occurred in the past at a specific time. This tense form is characterized by the use of the second-form verb (Verb-2).

Time Description :
• Yesterday
• Last... (Last week, last month, last year)
• ...ago (Two days ago, a year ago)
• This morning

Function A

Describe events that occurred in the past at a specific time.
Example: The students visited the National Monument last week.

Function B

Shows that an action took place within a certain period in the past.
Example: My father stayed in Qatar for five years.

Function C

Describe habits from the past.
Example: When I lived in Jakarta, I always bought soto betawi.

Function D

Shows past emotions.
Example: I was so happy when my parents came here.

Nominal Simple Past Tense (Non-Action)

Type Form Example
Positive (+) Subject + was/were + Complement I was a student.
Negative (-) Subject + was/were + not + Complement She was not a student.
Interrogative (?) Was/Were + Subject + Complement + ? Was she a student?

Note: Was (I, He, She, It) | Were (You, They, We)

Verbal Simple Past Tense (Action)

Type Form Example
Positive (+) Subject + V2 + Object She drank a cup of coffee.
Negative (-) Subject + did not + V1 + Object She did not drink a cup of coffee.
Interrogative (?) Did + Subject + V1 + Object + ? Did she drink a cup of coffee?
