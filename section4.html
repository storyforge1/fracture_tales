Narrative Text

Narrative text is a text that tells a story about an event chronologically. In general, a narrative story is an imaginative or unreal story that comes from the author imagination. The function of narrative text is to entertain the reader or audience.

General Structure of Narrative Text

1. Orientation (Pengenalan)
Introduces characters, time, and place.

2. Complication (Masalah)
A problem or conflict appears in the story.

3. Resolution (Penyelesaian)
The problem is solved (happy or sad ending).

Example: The Legend of Surabaya

🟡 Orientation
A long time ago, there were two animals named Sura and Baya. Sura was a shark, and Baya was a crocodile. They lived in the sea and were good friends.

🔴 Complication
One day, Sura and Baya were looking for food. Suddenly, they found a goat.
“This is my food!” said Baya.
“No, it is mine!” said Sura.

They argued and finally fought for the goat. After a long fight, they felt very tired. Then, they made an agreement to live in different places. Sura stayed in the sea, and Baya stayed on the land.

However, one day Sura broke the promise. He went to the river where Baya lived to look for food. Baya was very angry when he saw Sura. They fought again fiercely.

🟢 Resolution
In the end, they both hurt each other. Sura bit Baya’s tail, and Baya attacked Sura. Finally, Sura returned to the sea, and Baya stayed on the land. Since then, they never met again.

🎯 Explanation

Orientation → introduction of characters
Complication → conflict appears
Resolution → conflict solved
