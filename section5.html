Language Features of Narrative Text 📖

1. Action Verbs

Action verbs are words that show physical actions or activities done by the characters in a story. These verbs help readers understand what is happening and make the story more lively and interesting.

Common action verbs include: run, eat, go, fight, walk, see, take, bring.

Example:
Sura and Baya fought for the goat.
The boy ran quickly.

Action verbs are important because they make the story active and easy to imagine.

2. Simple Past Tense

Simple past tense is used to describe events or actions that happened in the past. Narrative texts usually use this tense because they tell stories that already happened.

Pattern: Subject + Verb 2

Examples:
go → went
eat → ate
fight → fought

Sentences:
They found a goat.
Sura went to the river.

Using past tense makes the story clear that the events happened before now.

3. Time Conjunction

Time conjunctions are words used to connect events in a story based on time order. They help the reader follow the sequence of events easily.

Common time conjunctions:
then, suddenly, after that, finally, next

Examples:
Suddenly, they saw a goat.
Then, they started to fight.

These words make the story organized and not confusing.

4. Dialogue

Dialogue is a conversation between characters in a story. It is usually written using quotation marks (“ ”). Dialogue helps show the characters’ feelings, thoughts, and interactions.

Examples:
“This is my food!” said Baya.
“No, it is mine!” said Sura.

Dialogue makes the story more real and interesting.

5. Descriptive Language

Descriptive language is used to describe characters, feelings, and situations in a story. It helps readers imagine the story clearly.

Examples:
angry, tired, happy, beautiful

Sentence:
Baya was very angry.
They felt tired after fighting.

This language makes the story more vivid and emotional.

6. Temporal Conjunction

Temporal conjunctions are words used to show time relationships between events in more detail.

Examples:
when, before, after, until, as soon as

Sentences:
When Sura came, Baya was angry.
After the fight, they rested.

These words help show clear connections between events.
